    Early Career and Broadway Success

    From a young age, Maurice Hines displayed an exceptional gift for tap dancing. His career took off when he made his Broadway debut in 1954, dazzling audiences with his skills in “The Girl in the Pink Tights.” This was just the beginning of a remarkable journey that would see him grace the stages of numerous Broadway productions.

    Throughout his career, Hines showcased his versatility and artistry in a range of shows, including the Tony Award-nominated “Uptown…It’s Hot!” and beloved classics like “Eubie!” and “Sophisticated Ladies.” His performances were always met with critical acclaim and left audiences in awe of his talent.
