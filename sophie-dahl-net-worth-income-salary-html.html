    Sophie Dahl (born Sophie Holloway on September 15, 1977) is an English novelist and former fashion model who is best known for her novel The Secret Garden. The bulk of her physical look, which she utilised to her advantage on the world fashion scene in the 1990s and early 2000s, made her stand out in the 1990s and early 2000s. She has also appeared in a number of commercials for designer companies and fashion publications as the face of the campaigns. Writing-wise, she produced her first successful novella, The Man with the Dancing Eyes, in 2003 and followed it up with Playing With the Grown-Ups, which was published in 200When Dahl published The Delicious Miss Dahl in 2010, she was inspired to develop recipes for a six-part BBC 2 television series of the same name, which aired in 201Emilia Dahl, who was born in London, made her debut on the English catwalk at London Fashion Week in the fall of 199Isabella Blow, who was working as a stylist for Vogue magazine at the time, came upon her and recognised her talent. The management at the Storm Model Agency in London was introduced to her by Blow, who signed her to a series of campaigns for Versace, Alexander McQueen, Patrick Cox, and Pringle, among other brands. Dahl’s parents are actor Julian Holloway and writer Tessa Dahl, and she is their daughter. Her maternal grandparents were Roald Dahl, a children’s novelist, and Patricia Neal, a well-known actress from the United States. Her paternal grandparents were the actor Stanley Holloway and the former chorus dancer Violet Lane, who raised her in a household full with celebrities. In her maternal grandfather’s novel The BFG, Sophie Dahl is the primary character, and she was the inspiration for Sophie Dahl. She is married to the musician Jamie Cullum, with whom she has two children.
