    Lil Loaded was born to American parents in the state of California.

    Net Worth, Albums, and Songs of Lil Loaded

    Thuggin Wit Me’s singer grew up listening to a variety of rappers. Bone Thugs-N-Harmony, Snoop Dogg, Tupac Shakur, Chief Keef, and Rich Homie Quan were among his favorite artists. They were mentioned by Loaded, and the town where he grew up influenced his sound. Lil Loaded made his debut as a rapper with the album B.O.S.

    All seemed to enjoy the album. The song has over 244k views on YouTube as of April 2020. Following that, he released another song called Pass In, but it didn’t perform as well as the first. Finally, he released 6locc 6a6y, his breakthrough single.

    The song, which had a raw street life element to it and reminding listeners of rapper Chief Keef, had the same raw street life element to it. The song’s video was filmed in the Dallas neighborhood where he was living at the time. He commented on the day the video was shot in an interview with Complex, stating,

    “There were fights and shit throughout the film. You can sense it. You can sense genuine electricity. That’s exactly what happened. That was just another day in the neighborhood.”

    Within eight months of its release, the song had amassed over 11 million views. Gang Unit, Opps On Fire, 6acc on the 6locc, and Out My Body are just a few of the well-known songs that have garnered him millions of YouTube views. On December 6, 2019, Lil Loaded released his first mixtape, 6locc 6a6y.

    When asked about his stage name, he commented on how it came about, saying he used to visit the basketball court with a gun in his backpack. And, he happily added, it was because of this that he was dubbed “Lil Loaded” by everyone.
