    Jay Chou is a Taiwanese musician, singer-songwriter, multi-instrumentalist, actor, and director.

    He was born on January 18, 1979, in Linkou, Taipei County, Taiwan. Chou is known for his unique style of blending traditional Chinese music with Western hip-hop and R&B, which has earned him numerous awards and accolades throughout his career.

    Chou first rose to fame in the early 2000s with his debut album, “Jay,” which became an instant hit in Asia. He has since released over a dozen more albums, including “Fantasy,” “Ye Hui Mei,” and “The Era.” Chou has also acted in several movies, including “Initial D,” “Curse of the Golden Flower,” and “The Green Hornet.”

    Jay Chou’s top movies and TV shows

    Movies: Secret (2007) – This romantic drama film, which Chou directed and starred in, tells the story of a young pianist who falls in love with a girl who plays the violin.

    Initial D (2005) – This action-packed film is based on a popular manga series and follows a young street racer as he competes against other drivers in dangerous mountain races.

    Jay Chou awards and nominations

    Jay has won the Golden Melody Award, which is the most prestigious music award in Taiwan, 23 times. This includes Best Mandarin Male Singer, Best Album Producer, and Best Mandarin Album.

    He has also won the World Music Award for Best Selling Chinese Artist three times. In 2013, Jay won the MTV Europe Music Award for Best Worldwide Act, becoming the first Chinese artist to win the award. He has been nominated for the Grammy Awards twice, in 2016 and 2019, for his work on albums by other artists.
