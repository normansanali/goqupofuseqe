    Gen. Jack Keane is a former Army general who served in the United States Army. He has also served in the United States Army as Vice Chief of Staff.

    In addition, Jack has been awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom. In addition, he works as a national security analyst.

    Keane is the chairman of the Institute for the Study of War at the moment. Aside from that, he also serves as chairman of AM General.

    Early childhood development and education

    Keane, the son of Elizabeth (Davis) and John Keane, was born in Manhattan, New York, in 1943. Ronald is his partner.

    Keane graduated with a bachelor’s degree in accounting from Fordham University, where he was a member of the Pershing Rifles. After that, he went to Western Kentucky University and earned a master’s degree in philosophy. He went on to the Army War College and the Command and General Staff College after that.

    Military service

    In 2002, Keane stands by as President George W. Bush signs a defense appropriations bill.

    Keane was a paratrooper in the Vietnam War, serving as a platoon leader and company commander. The Combat Infantry Badge and the Master Parachutist Badge are also available to him. Later, he participated in US operations in Somalia, Haiti, Bosnia, and Herzegovina, and Kosovo. The 1st Brigade, 10th Mountain Division, 101st Airborne Division, and XVIII Airborne Corps are among his commands.

    During a live-fire exercise in 1991, Keane saved David Petraeus’ life. Petraeus was fired, according to Keane “I had to fight to save his life because he was standing right next to me by mistake. He had a quarter-sized hole in his back that was gushing blood, and we stopped the bleeding, got him on a helicopter, and took him to a surgeon, and we’ve been kind of bonded since then.”

    In 2003, Keane stepped down from the military.

     awards

    Two Defense Distinguished Service Medals, two Army Distinguished Service Medals, the Silver Star, five Legion of Merits, the Bronze Star Medal, three Meritorious Service Medals, one Army Commendation Medal, the Joint Chiefs Service Badge, the Humanitarian Service Medal,[3] Ranger Tab, Combat Infantryman Badge, Master Parachutist Badge, and Air Assault Badge are among the military awards Keane has earned.

    On March 10, 2020, President Donald Trump awarded Keane the Presidential Medal of Freedom.

    The Fordham University Distinguished Alumni Award, the USO 2002 Man of the Year award, and the Association of the United States Army 2001 Man of the Year award are among his civilian honors.
